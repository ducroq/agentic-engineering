# ADR-005: Augmented Engineering Is a Tool Umbrella, Not a Research Proposition

**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Status:** Accepted

## Context

The project started as a research proposition: "What's new when engineers work with AI agents?" — four patterns extracted from nine projects. This generated valuable artifacts: agent-ready-projects (a framework), layered verification insights, five case studies, a claim registry, and a website presenting the research.

But the most impactful artifact is agent-ready-projects — a concrete, adoptable tool. The proposition itself is a snapshot of one researcher's observations, with acknowledged limitations (N=1, survivorship bias, echo chamber risk). The academic apparatus (claim registry, confidence tiers, typed verification) signals "research" to an audience that wants "tools I can use."

## Decision

Reframe "Augmented Engineering" as an umbrella for durable tools and patterns for engineers working with AI agents. The four-pattern research proposition becomes backstory — it generated the tools, but the tools are the point.

## Consequences

**Positive:**
- Clear identity: tools for practitioners, not a research thesis to defend
- Room to grow: new tools join the umbrella without fitting a "four patterns" frame
- agent-ready-projects is already the flagship — this aligns framing with reality
- Removes pressure to maintain research rigor on what is ultimately practitioner wisdom

**Negative:**
- The proposition and case studies lose their central role — they served their purpose but become archival
- The claim registry and confidence calibration infrastructure may no longer justify maintenance
- The podcast framing ("four patterns") may need to evolve

## Alternatives Considered

- **Keep the research framing**: Rejected — the useful output is tools, not the proposition. The research framing creates maintenance burden and signals the wrong audience.
- **Abandon the umbrella entirely, just maintain agent-ready-projects**: Considered but rejected — layered verification is a credible second tool, and the umbrella name is already established (domain, podcast, repo).
