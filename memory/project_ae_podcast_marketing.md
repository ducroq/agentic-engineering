## Series Identity

**Title**: Agentic Engineering: the craft, not the hype
**Core question**: What's genuinely new when humans and AI agents build things together?
**Format**: Three-voice scripted podcast (Lisa/Marc/Sven) targeting local TTS (Chatterbox, Qwen3-TTS, TADA)
**Term origin**: "Agentic engineering" coined by Andrej Karpathy (Feb 8 2026), popularized by Addy Osmani. Generic descriptor, not trademarked. Widely used.

## Proposition / Promise

**One-liner**: What's genuinely new when humans and AI agents build things together — from someone who actually did the work.

**Promise to listener**:
- Learn what works and what doesn't from real projects, not conference talks
- We'll tell you when something is just old engineering in a new hat
- We'll tell you when we don't know — N=1 honesty, no "10x productivity" claims
- Every episode gives you something you can try tomorrow

**Tagline**: "The craft, not the hype"

**The deeper story** (from the creator's own experience):
- "I built things I couldn't have built at all without AI" — this is a capability shift, not a productivity metric. The comparison doesn't exist. You can't measure time saved on something you never would have attempted.
- Nine projects across hardware, academic writing, news, embedded systems, podcasts, education — one person. That's not productivity improvement. That's a new kind of engineer.
- But the cognitive load is the highest it's ever been. Five projects simultaneously means five mental models, five sets of constraints, five context architectures — in your head. The agent does the work. You carry the weight of knowing whether the work is right.
- Both are true at the same time: catapulted AND loaded. This honest duality is the series' emotional core.

## Window Assessment (March 2026)

- The window for "first context engineering podcast" is closing — Harrison Chase, Build Wiz, Microsoft are all touching the topic.
- The window for "honest practitioner podcast" is NOT closing — nobody wants that position because it's hard to produce and less marketable.
- Don't rush to ship mediocre audio. Quality is the moat. The scripts are strong, the review battery validated them.
- The content creator reviewer said "ship before the window closes" but the podcast listener said "better than 80% of SE Daily." That quality gap matters more than speed.

## Tandemize.ai Funnel Strategy

**Concept**: The podcast serves as top-of-funnel for tandemize.ai — a business being started by a friend around agentic engineering consulting/services.

**Funnel flow**:
1. **Podcast** builds credibility and audience ("the craft, not the hype")
2. **Listeners** recognize the problems in their own teams
3. **tandemize.ai** is where teams go when they want help implementing it

**tandemize.ai name assessment**:
- Strong concept — "tandem" = human + AI working together
- "-ize" suffix differentiates from crowded "Tandem" space
- "Tandemize" as verb is memorable and unclaimed
- Key risk: SEO competition with other "Tandem AI" companies

**Strongest funnel topics** (per review battery):
- Team scaling problem — solo devs can DIY but teams need guidance
- The cognitive load — managers need frameworks for sustainable AI adoption
- Verification economics — teams need systematic approaches, not individual heroics

**How to apply**: Episodes that expose problems individuals can't solve alone are natural funnel content for tandemize.ai. Marc's "I don't have a clean answer for teams yet" is the honest version of a sales lead.
